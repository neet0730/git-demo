Yusan Lin

Hi! I am a fourth-year Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, an instructor for Database Management Systems. I passed my comprehensive exam in summer 2015 and am officially A.B.D (All But Defense). My research is in Data Mining, Natural Language Processing, and Social Network Analysis.

About

I was born in Hsinchu, Taiwan and I went to National Hsinchu Girls' Senior High School for an engineering education. I left Hsinchu to pursue a Bachelor degree in Computer Science and Information Engineering at National Central University (NCU). While studying at NCU, I also worked as a freelance model, and was offered a modeling contract. However, due to my determination to pursue further study in the United States, I turned down the contract and began my Ph.D. studies in 2012.

I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, and I'm undertaking research with Dr. Wang-Chien Lee in iPDA lab.

Research

My research interests lie in the fields of Data Mining (Text Mining, in particular), Natural Language Processing, and Social Network Analysis. I have combined my knowledge in these fields with my outside interests to form my current research topics, thanks to my wonderful adviser, mentors, and collaborators.

I am a member of the iPDA research group, supervised by Dr. Wang-Chien Lee. I am also in charge of the Big Data team in iPDA, working on projects through Big Data approaches (we embrace Hadoop and Spark).

I joined Trendalytics Innovation Labs Inc as a research intern in summer 2015.
